# 2026 Summer EarthRISE DEVELOP Philadelphia Health & Air Quality

# Description
Heat is the leading cause of weather-related deaths in the United States. The effects of heat are exacerbated by the urban heat island phenomenon, which creates relatively higher temperatures in cities compared to rural and undeveloped areas. This project supports the City of Philadelphia Office of Sustainability and Office of Public Health, Division of Disease Control by creating updated and usable heat exposure data for Philadelphia. Remotely sensed land surface temperature, albedo, and vegetation data were calculated from Landsat 5,7,8, and 9, NOAA-20/J1 VIIRS, Sentinel-2, and Aqua MODIS. With more recent and robust data for LST and heat mitigating factors in the Philadelphia area, the city is interested in updating their heat vulnerability index map to improve their understanding of current local heat patterns and how this has changed over the last 20 years. Partners intend to incorporate additional local and demographic data to evaluate community impacts of extreme urban heat events and identify areas in need of targeted heat mitigation interventions.
